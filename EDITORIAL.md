# fellipebrito.com — EDITORIAL

Editor's audit of all 112 essays. Initial pass: 2026-04-28.

A working document for the **editorial track**: which essays are LinkedIn-ready, which need real editing, which patterns to lean into, which to retire. Distinct from `ROADMAP.md` (which is about building new surfaces). This is about polishing the existing body of work and selecting from it for amplification.

## Portrait — who emerges from 112 pieces

Fellipe writes like an insider who walked into the church machinery, watched it up close, and walked back out — refusing to walk back in on the old terms. Not bitter ex-vangelical. Not cool agnostic. Stubbornly, costly faithful.

His mind is recursive and dialectical. He thinks by writing **letters** — to his anxiety, to "dear church", to Júlio César, to Ted Scott across a gallery rope, to himself at 20. The signature move is **third-option thinking**: he keeps refusing the binaries he's handed.

The questions that haunt the corpus: *Have I become my idols?* *Can the church be the church without becoming a brand?* *Is faith reasonable for an engineer?* *Am I arriving anywhere or just moving?*

Underneath everything: an expat's loneliness. The boy from a fishing village in southern Brazil who left at 20 keeps scoring the same wound. The Augusta hug from `we-never-lose` and the 1995 Flamengo hug from the same essay — same hug. Same essay, twice.

## Three pieces worth real editing

Underlying idea is strong, execution lets it down.

- **`our-father`** — Premise is gold (subway exit at Wall Street, "OUR father" expanding into a litany of strangers). It just stops. Add three more concrete fathers (the woman in the wheelchair, the cop, the kid begging) and a closing turn that returns to the writer — *and OUR father, of me, who didn't want to share this prayer with any of them.*
- **`i-think-therefore-i-believe`** — Real intellectual scaffolding (Crick, Sagan, Keller, Lewis), buried under section headers. Cut every header. Pick the *one* fine-tuning argument that broke open for him personally and tell that story; let the others become a footnote. Right now it reads like sermon notes; it could be a confession.
- **`just-keep-swimming`** — Great Mark 4 reading ("Jesus didn't expect them to calm the storm; he expected them to use the bucket and tie the rope"), then ends with a cut-off Nemo reference that didn't render. Rewrite the ending: paint the bucket, the rope, the boat angle. Show what "keep sailing" looks like for an entrepreneur in 2026, not for a fisherman in 30 AD.

## Patterns to lean into (the underused superpowers)

1. **Story-first → meaning emerges.** When he opens with a scene (the airplane ringing, the keyboard meltdown in Hollywood, the boy on his father's lap in '95), the writing flies. Every piece that works has this shape.
2. **Letter form.** `a-conversation-with-my-anxiety`, `a-letter-to-a-dear-friend`, `a-letter-to-julio-cesar`, `our-father` — his most honest register. Write more letters and label them as such.
3. **Cross-pollinated worlds.** Tolkien × John 1. U2 × the Kingdom. Senna × Paul. FIFA × Christmas. Augusta × Brazil. Most interesting when his tech / golf / music / Bible lanes collide.

## Patterns to retire

1. **The italic prayer-outro.** "*May we be wise. May the God of love and grace forgive us all.*" Twenty pieces end this way. It deflates lived stories into a sermon coda. Cut these endings — let the scene be the ending.
2. **Sermonic bullet-list essays.** `i-think-therefore-i-believe`, `what-difference-does-it-make-if-god-exists`, `values-of-a-family-without-god` — argument-by-headers with "Therefore, life has not:" filler. Same content delivered as one specific story would be 5× stronger.
3. **The "I'm too radical" tic.** Repeats almost verbatim in five pieces. It's a defensive crouch — he braces for criticism inside the piece. Stop bracing. State the position.

## Category ranking

1. **Golf** — strongest. Three pieces, all originals, all sensory and earned. Golf forces him to inhabit the body; the body keeps the prose honest.
2. **Bible** — strongest *intellect*, uneven *delivery*. Standouts (`a-message-from-the-courtyard`, `i-am-recovering-the-divine-voice`, `the-light-the-darkness-cannot-understand`, `the-case-for-christian-skepticism`, `harry-potter-and-christmas`) are bracingly fresh.
3. **Music** — slight category. Most pieces are song quotes with light framing — playlist liner notes more than essays.
4. **Free Thought / Short Stories** — weakest as a coherent body. Best ones (`30-years`, `i-am-not-a-billboard`, `our-father`) are great; the category also collects throwaways.

## On the Brazilian evangelical church polemics — recalibrated

**Initial editorial note** (mine and the audit's): "stop writing about the Brazilian evangelical church for a year — it's costing you the Augusta voice."

**Fellipe's pushback** (correct): "These are writings of the last 15 years. You read it all as if I wrote it this month. There are years there, and many years since the last time I wrote about that."

**Recalibration:**

- The eight polemic pieces (`brazil-before-the-throne`, `the-curse-of-religion`, `jesus-rich`, `unconvert-yourself`, `malafaia-and-the-lack-of-friends`, `for-the-love-of-lost-causes`, `its-unbearable`, `those-who-have-a-promise-dont-die`) are an **archived chapter**, not a current pattern. The diagnosis was right; the call to "stop" was moot — Fellipe already moved on.
- The reframe: when introducing the corpus to a new reader, **frame it as a 15-year arc**. Some of it is who he was; some is who he's becoming. The first-person intro on `/blog/all` does this explicitly.
- For new writing: the Augusta voice is the front. The polemic mode is in the rear-view mirror.
- For LinkedIn amplification: don't surface the polemic body unless tied to a current event. The seven LinkedIn picks above intentionally exclude that body — not because it's bad, but because it's not where the writer is now.
