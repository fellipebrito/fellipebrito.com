Atheism, in a broad sense, is the absence of belief in the existence of deities. Atheism is the opposite of theism, which in its most general form is the belief that at least one deity exists.

Now, why is it that 95% of so-called atheists on social media behave as opposed to religion, and with little or no evidence of their belief?

I see atheists exercise their "preaching" using isolated Bible texts to demonstrate the "evil," "questionable character," and even "errors" of God. Wouldn't this attitude itself be proof of the existence of, for better or worse, a god — whose very existence would knock down any veracity of atheism?

Look, I respect the right to believe in something, but I think that just as the evangelical crowd "makes me feel sorry" because they're so confused, in the same way these so-called atheists are nothing more than rebels without a cause, with very little faith — whether to combat mine or to protect their own.
